Some common ice maker issues and their potential fixes:

Not producing ice

If your ice machine is not producing ice, the first thing to check is the water supply. Make sure the water supply valve is turned on and providing sufficient water pressure. If the water supply is good, the problem may be a clogged water filter, a malfunctioning water inlet valve, or a faulty ice machine control board. These issues typically require professional repair.

Producing small or hollow ice cubes:

If your ice machine is producing small or hollow ice cubes, the problem may be low water pressure or a dirty water filter. Check the water pressure and replace the water filter if necessary. If the problem persists, call a professional to inspect and repair the water inlet valve.

Producing too much ice:

If your ice machine is producing too much ice, the problem may be a faulty ice level control sensor or a malfunctioning ice thickness control sensor. A professional can diagnose and repair these issues.

Ice tastes bad:

If your ice tastes bad or has an unusual odor, the problem may be a dirty water filter, a dirty evaporator plate, or a dirty ice bin. Replace the water filter and clean the evaporator plate and ice bin to resolve this issue.

Leaking water:

If your ice machine is leaking water, the problem may be a clogged or frozen water line, a faulty water inlet valve, or a damaged water pump. Check the water line and inlet valve for damage, and call a professional to repair or replace any damaged components.

Loud noises:

If your ice machine is making loud or unusual noises, the problem may be a loose or damaged component such as a fan blade or motor. A professional can diagnose and repair these issues.
