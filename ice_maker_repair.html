Some common ice maker issues and their potential fixes:

Not producing ice

If your ice maker is not producing ice, there are several possible causes. First, check the water supply to ensure it is turned on and providing sufficient water pressure. If the water supply is good, the problem may be a clogged or frozen water line, a faulty water inlet valve, or a malfunctioning ice maker motor. These issues typically require professional repair.

Producing small or hollow ice cubes:

If your ice maker is producing small or hollow ice cubes, the problem may be low water pressure, a clogged water filter, or a faulty water inlet valve. Check the water pressure and replace the water filter if necessary. If the problem persists, call a professional to inspect and repair the water inlet valve.

Producing too much ice:

If your ice maker is producing too much ice, the problem may be a faulty ice maker switch or a malfunctioning ice maker thermostat. A professional can diagnose and repair these issues.

Ice tastes bad:

If your ice tastes bad or has an unusual odor, the problem may be a dirty water filter or a dirty ice maker. Replace the water filter and clean the ice maker to resolve this issue.

Leaking water:

If your ice maker is leaking water, the problem may be a clogged or frozen water line, a faulty water inlet valve, or a damaged ice maker mold. Check the water line and inlet valve for damage, and call a professional to repair or replace any damaged components.
